## 1. Project Overview
This document summarizes the verified human normative data, scientific taxonomies, and clinical protocols established for the **Cognitive Auditory Benchmark (CAB)**.

## 3. Scientific Taxonomies
We established that the CAB tasks map onto two primary scientific frameworks: the **Clinical CAP Model** (diagnostic) and the **ELU Model** (cognitive load).

### A. Clinical Taxonomy (Chermak & Musiek Model)
*Source: Davidson & Souza (2024)*

| Category | Definition | Relevant CAB Task |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Temporal Processing** | Processing time-related cues (ordering, duration). | • PASAT (Speed)<br>• Interrupted Speech (Gating) |
| **Dichotic Processing** | Processing competing signals in both ears. | • Forced-Left Dichotic Listening |
| **Low Redundancy** | Recognizing degraded/distorted speech (Auditory Closure). | • Time-Compressed Speech<br>• Reverberant Speech |
| **Binaural Interaction** | Brainstem integration of binaural cues (localization). | • Masking Level Difference (MLD) |
| **Discrimination** | Detecting subtle acoustic differences. | • Frequency/Pitch Discrimination |

### B. The ELU Model (Ease of Language Understanding)
*Source: Rönnberg et al.*

* **Implicit Loop:** Used for **Clear Speech**. Automatic, effortless matching of phonemes to Long-Term Memory.
* **Mismatch Trigger:** Occurs in **QuickSIN** and **Reverberant Speech**. The signal is distorted, preventing automatic matching.
* **Explicit Loop:** Engaged by **PASAT** and **3-Back**. Requires conscious Working Memory to "repair" the signal or hold information for manipulation.

## 4. Clinical Protocols & Prompts
To accurately reproduce these benchmarks, the following standardized patient prompts were identified:

* **Temporal Patterning:** "You will hear three tones (High/Low). Tell me the pattern you hear, like **'High-Low-High'**."
* **Dichotic Separation:** "Ignore the numbers in your left ear and tell me **only** the numbers you hear in your right ear."
* **Time-Compressed Speech:** "You will hear words that sound like they are spoken very quickly. Repeat the word, **even if you have to guess**."
* **Electrophysiology (ABR):** "Sit back and relax. You will hear clicking sounds, but you **do not need to respond**."
